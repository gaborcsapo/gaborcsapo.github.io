About me

Hi! I’m Gabor, a Computer Science major studying at New York University Abu Dhabi. I’m from Hungary, but thanks to my studies I lived for two years in the Middle East, for one semester in Shanghai, China and soon I’ll be moving to New York. I didn’t always know that I wanted to be a Computer Scientist, but in my freshman year I realized that my passion lies in creation and building. Since then I’ve learnt a great deal about designing efficient software in class and in my free-time. I’ve had internships in Africa, in Germany and in the UAE, while I also work as a freelancer mainly making websites. I’m always out for an adventure, I love hiking, surfing and wakeboarding. I’m grateful to NYUAD for giving me the opportunity to travel to 15+ countries in the past 3 years of which I tried to make the best use of by learning about different cultures and life in many places.

Work Experience

Software Engineer at CTED

Ghana and Abu Dhabi - Summer 2016 - 3 months

The Center for Technology and Economic Development is a research center in Ghana, New York and the UAE that focuses on the development of novel tech that can impact economic development in under-developed regions. My task was to extend their web apps built with Node.js and Socket.io. While interning with them I developed the prototype of an SMS-based taxi application that will likely be continued by the team. The app runs on Android. I was invited to their center in Africa for the summer, but I also worked with them in the UAE.

Webdesigner at BlitzBringer

Frankfurt - Summer 2015 - 2 months

Blitzbringer is a startup in Frankfurt, Germany working on a crowdsourced delivery service. I worked as an intern in a team of 5 people. My main task was to build a new website for the company, which unfortunately they changed this summer. I certainly learnt a lot about web design, business, marketing, but the real learning outcomes were about life. I moved to a completely new city with zero help, zero people I knew and zero money and I had find a way to make the most out of what I had, which was a pleasant challenge.

Freelance

Since Fall 2014

Side Projects

DIYDictionary

This is a selfish project. When I moved a foreign language environment, I met countless new words that I wanted to learn myself. First, I wrote them on pieces of papers, but I kept losing them. I wished there had been an app to store the words electronically… Then I realized I’m a CS major, why not make one for myself?!? I was introduced at a Hackathon in Shanghai to Hybrid Apps. To try them out I used the Ionic Framework with AngularJS and SQLite to accomplish the task I set myself. I have a huge list of plans how to develop the project into a commercially available app.

Game Design

I like to have fun while I’m learning and the easiest way to do so is to design games. My very first program was a copy of the Snake game that I used to play on my Dad’s Nokia. I also created a 2D Creativity Puzzle Game, that requires some serious brain power and the demolishment of mental walls to solve. I created that project to learn about Processing and animations. In my Software Engineering class we focused on software design patterns. We learnt about the Factory, Singleton, MVC, Decorator and Observer patterns by creating board and card games.

Visual Design

Graphic design is a crucial part of development. People decide whether to buy/use/watch a product in 3 seconds. We have to acknowledge that humans are visual mammals who judge by the looks. It is important to have an eye for good design to please every audience. I contributed to logo, printout, website designs and a marketing campaign for the Burj Khalifa in Dubai. My style for the web is minimalist but I always want to spice it up with some unique ideas, which is true for most of the things I do. I often get inspiration from the great artists of Bauhaus like Laszlo Moholy-Nagy.

Interactive Websites

The web holds countless opportunities to create web applications that leverage public data and digital services from a wide range of existing web products. I have played around with the Instagram API, Google Maps API, Twitter API, Three.js, P5.js, CouchDB and many more things to design some interesting web experiences.

Amateur Travel Photography

For now I’m mostly using my humble phone since I like travelling super light, but as the saying goes: not the camera makes the good photographer… If you’re interested, I put together an album with some of my pictures.
